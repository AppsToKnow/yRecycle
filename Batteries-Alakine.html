DEFINITION & USE
Alkaline batteries are batteries that work with the reaction between zinc and manganese dioxide. A rechargeable alkaline battery allows reuse of specially designed cells. Alkaline batteries account for 80% of manufactured batteries in the US and over 10 B individual units produced worldwide. Alkaline batteries are in many household items such as MP3 players, CD players, digital cameras, pagers, toys, lights, and radios, to just name a few.

RECYCLE
Single-use batteries contain materials that are both recyclable and considered Dangerous to the environment. You can drop them off at a household hazmat (HHW) facility or participate in the many mail-in or take-back programs that are available all over the country.

TRASH
The batteries no longer contain mercury, so they can now safely be disposed with regular waste.
