DEFINITION & USE
It is a paint made with oil such as linseed oil, poppy seed oil, walnut oil, and safflower oil. It has some hazardous and flammable chemicals such as: Volatile Organic Compounds and many others.

CREATE
It is made with a coloring matter called pigment, solvent, binder, additives. Youtube video “How it’s Made” ("https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pgir0J00JaM")

RECYCLE
Contact the Household Hazardous Waste facility in your area or your nearest Recycling Coordinator. Many places will recycle oil based paints.

TRASH
Always try to use up all the paint. DO NOT dump paint down the sink. (In most states it is illegal to throw oil based paint away!) If these chemicals are introduced to our water systems people may get sick. Side effects of VOC are : Eyes, Nose, and Throat irritation, Headaches,Nausea/Vomiting, Dizziness, Worsening of Asthma symptoms. If given a lot of VOC you can get : Cancer, Liver damage, Kidney damage, Central Nervous System damage. If you can’t use it on a project, paint it onto some cardboard and let it dry. Make sure the paint is totally dry. Check the label to see if any hazardous material is in it. Often times there is. If this is the case you must contact the Household Hazardous Waste facility in your area. If not, you can safely throw it away.
