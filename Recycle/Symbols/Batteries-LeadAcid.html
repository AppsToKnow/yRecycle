DEFINITION & USE
A secondary battery in which the electrodes and grids of lead are immersed in dilute sulfuric acid solution.

Cars, boats, and other public transportation vehicles use these batteries for energy . And in a power outage or a blackout they give energy to hospitals so the patients are safe.

CREATE
Lead acid is melted, stirred and atomised then added with oxygen to create lead oxide. It stores energy in chemical form in 62 vault cells. Stored in sulfuric acid.

RECYCLE
Most of them can be recycled. When recycled it needs less energy but gives of the same amount. When you go buy your new battery just give your old one to the retailer.

TRASH
Do not throw into the trash. When you go buy your new battery just give your old one to the retailer
