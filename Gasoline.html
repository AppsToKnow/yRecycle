DEFINITION & USE
Gasoline is a fuel made from petroleum. It is mostly made from crude oil (remains of plants and animals) from hundreds of millions of years ago. Most gasoline is used in cars and light trucks. You can also fuel boats, recreational vehicles, and landscaping equipment with gasoline.

The most common form of gasoline used now is MTBE (methyl tertiary-butyl ether). It is colorless, flammable, and has a strong odor. The chemical formula is C5H12O. Health Hazards- You usually come into contact with gasoline by breathing it, drinking or eating something that has been contaminated, or just touching it. Breathing a large amount of gasoline could cause you to have lung or nose irritation. It could also cause dizziness or a headache. If you were to swallow it, it would give you stomach irritation. Drinking it could result in death. If gasoline were to get into the groundwater people could be exposed to it by either drinking it or breathing it. This could happen while cooking, bathing, showering, and drinking from a contaminated water source. Some of it can be absorbed through the skin.
