DEFINITION & USE
An incandescent light bulb is a light bulb in which an electric current passes through a filament , heating it up until it becomes incandescent, producing light. This type of light bulb can be found in lamps and overhead lights.

CREATE
An incandescent light is made by putting two metal contacts and they connect to a metal filament. An electric current passes through the filament, heating it to a temperature that produces light.

Video from The Science Channel: http://www.sciencechannel.com/tv-shows/how-its-made/videos/how-its-made-light-bulb.htm

RECYCLE
Incandescent light bulbs can not be recycled but it can be thrown away.

TRASH
Most cities do not accept incandescent light bulbs in their recycling programs so you can dispose of them in the trash but because they break easy you should wrap them in paper or a different wrapping protection.

In a landfill it takes about 300 years to decompose!
